Research Notes

There is a lot of propaganda out there about we hit the job numbers, or we missed the numbers.

Same thing with the JOLTS report that says there are ten million jobs out there.

I wanted to know what our history was so I could cut through the propaganda.

Over the last 20 years I learned that there are three methods the Department of Labor uses to track employment/unemployment.

They are the CES, CPS, and OES.

Problem is, they are all based on samples and not actual numbers as I came to find out.

The reports I show here take their government data, put them in a table and chart that data as well as show how many jobs we have gained between the years shown.

I have found once source which actually counts every 1040 based employee and it is the IRS Databook.

In 2020 we had 154,094,555 (page 4) and in 2000 we had 127,590,000 (page 6).

That is 26,504,555 jobs gained between these two years.

That is 1,325,228 jobs per year.

How can there be 10 million jobs out there if we only create about 1.3 million jobs per year?

Do you understand now why I have spent the last 20 years studying these numbers?

I'm not interested in discussing the politics of the situation, but I do want to discuss the math.

When we send a job to another country, it decreases the jobs available here at home by one and makes us dependent on another country for that job.

If it were one of our better paying jobs, it also decreases the opportuntiy for our citizens that want to acquire the skills to work their way up the ladder.

And if we import a non-immigrant guest worker at the same time, it actually means we have lost 2 jobs with one going offshore and one more lost here at home.

If these both are our best paying jobs, it destroys the ability of two Americans to strive for a better life.
